Tarte Tatin recipe

Description

Tarte Tatin is a pastry in which the fruit (usually apples)
is caramelized in butter and sugar before the tart is baked.
Named after the Tatin sisters who invented it and served it in their hotel as its signature dish, it originated in France
but has spread to other countries over time.

A popular myth tells that the dessert was accidentally created at the Hôtel Tatin in central France in the 1880s.

The hotel was run by two sisters, Stéphanie and Caroline Tatin.
There are a few myths about the tart's origin, the most common is that Stéphanie Tatin, who did most of the cooking, was tired one day and
tried to make a traditional apple pie, but she left the apples cooking in butter and sugar for too long.
Smelling the burning, she tried to save the dish by putting the pastry base on top of the pan of apples
and quickly finishing the cooking by putting the whole pan in the oven.

After serving the upside-down tart, she was surprised to find how much the hotel guests liked the dessert.
In a different version of the myth, Stéphanie baked a caramelized apple tart upside-down by mistake

Ingredients

Flaky Pastry

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 110g unsalted butter, cold, cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons ice water

Apple Filling

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 140g unsalted butter, cut into thin slices
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 kg apples peeled and halved
  • Crème fraîche, whipped cream, or ice cream, for serving

Steps

Make the Flaky Pastry

  1. Blend the flour with the salt in a food processor.
  2. Add the butter and process until well blended, about 8 seconds.
  3. Add the water and process just until the water has been absorbed and the mixture looks like wet sand.
  4. Transfer the pastry to a work surface and knead lightly until it comes together.
  5. Pat the dough into a disk, wrap in plastic and refrigerate until firm, about 1 hour.
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll the pastry into an 11 1/2-inch round. Drape the dough over a rolling pin, then open it out on a baking sheet lined with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to use.

Meanwhile, make the apple filling

  1. Spread the sugar evenly in the bottom of a 9-inch cast-iron skillet. Scatter the butter over the sugar and drizzle with the vanilla.
  2. Arrange the apple halves on their sides in the skillet in 2 concentric circles with all of the apples facing the same direction.
    Pack the apples closely together. Place 1 apple half in the center to fill the empty space.
  3. Set the skillet over moderately low heat and cook the apples until the surrounding syrup becomes a thick, golden brown, 45 minutes to 1 hour.
    Baste the apples with the syrup regularly with a bulb baster. The liquid should remain at a gentle bubble.
  4. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  5. Place the skillet on a baking sheet and bake the apples for 15 minutes. Remove from the oven.
  6. Drape the Flaky Pastry over the apples and carefully push the edge of the pastry down inside the pan.
  7. Return the skillet to the oven and bake for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and the juices are bubbling over.
  8. Remove the tart from the oven. Immediately invert a platter with a lip over the skillet.
    Quickly and carefully invert the tart onto the platter so the apples are on top.
    If any apples stick to the skillet, set them back into the tart.
  9. Serve warm or at room temperature, with dollops of crème fraîche, whipped cream, or ice cream.
